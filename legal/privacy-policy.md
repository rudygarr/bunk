# CampHQ Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** [DATE]
**Last updated:** [DATE]

[Company Legal Name] ("CampHQ," "we," "us," or "our") provides software that helps
camp organizers run overnight and day camps — managing rosters, transportation,
lodging, schedules, announcements, and related logistics (the "Service"). This
Privacy Policy explains how we handle personal information.

## 1. The most important thing: who controls the data

CampHQ is a tool that **camp organizers** (such as a school, church, ministry, or
other organization — the "Organizer") use to run their camp. The Organizer
decides what information to collect about participants and why.

- For information about camp participants (campers, parents/guardians,
  volunteers, and staff), the **Organizer is the data controller** and CampHQ is
  a **service provider / data processor** acting on the Organizer's instructions.
- For the Organizer's own account information and our billing relationship with
  the Organizer, **CampHQ is the controller.**

This means the Organizer — not CampHQ — is responsible for having the legal right
and any required consents (including **verifiable parental consent for children
under 13** and any school-records consents) to collect participant information and
enter it into the Service. See Section 5.

## 2. Who this policy covers

- **Organizers and their staff/admins** who create accounts and set up camps.
- **Participants** whose information an Organizer enters or who sign themselves
  up: campers, parents/guardians, volunteers, and adult staff.
- **Viewers** who open a public camp link without an account.

## 3. Information we collect

**a. Organizer account information.** Name, email, phone, organization name,
password (stored only as a salted hash), and billing details. Payment card
numbers are collected and processed by **Stripe**, our payment processor — CampHQ
never sees or stores full card numbers.

**b. Camp/participant information entered by Organizers or participants.**
Depending on how the Organizer configures their camp, this may include: name,
email or phone, grade/age, gender, bunkmate/friend requests, bus/cabin/team/group
assignments, attendance and roll-call status, schedule, and announcements.

**c. Health and emergency information.** If the Organizer enables it, this may
include allergies, medications, dietary needs, and emergency-contact names and
phone numbers. We treat this as **sensitive information** and use it only to
provide the Service to that camp.

**d. Photos.** Organizers may upload photos or — recommended — link to an
external shared album (e.g., iCloud or Google Photos) that they host themselves.
Where an external album is linked, those photos are governed by that provider's
terms, not ours.

**e. Technical/usage information.** Standard log data such as device/browser
type, IP address, and in-app actions, used to operate and secure the Service.
We do **not** sell personal information or use it for third-party advertising.

## 4. How we use information

- To provide, maintain, and secure the Service for the Organizer's camp.
- To authenticate users and enforce access permissions.
- To process the Organizer's payments and send service-related messages
  (e.g., verification codes, invitations, and notifications the Organizer sends).
- To provide support, prevent abuse, and comply with law.

We process participant information only to provide the Service to the Organizer
and on the Organizer's behalf.

## 5. Children's privacy (COPPA / FERPA)

The Service is **not directed to children** for the purpose of CampHQ collecting
their data for our own use, and children do not transact with CampHQ directly.

- An Organizer who enters or invites a child's information represents that they
  have obtained any **verifiable parental consent** required by the Children's
  Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and any other applicable law.
- Where an Organizer is a **school or acting on a school's behalf**, camp
  information may constitute education records under the Family Educational Rights
  and Privacy Act (FERPA); CampHQ acts as a **"school official"** with a
  legitimate educational interest, uses the data only to provide the Service,
  and does not re-disclose it except as permitted.
- We use children's information **only** to provide the Service to the camp and
  do not use it to build profiles, advertise, or sell.
- A parent/guardian may contact the **Organizer** to review, correct, or delete
  their child's information; we will assist the Organizer with any such request.

If you believe a child's information has been provided to us without proper
consent, contact us at **[privacy@yourdomain]** and we will work with the
Organizer to delete it.

## 6. When we share information

We share information only as needed to run the Service:

- **With the Organizer** and the users they authorize (e.g., a bus captain sees
  their bus roster), according to the permissions the Organizer sets.
- **With service providers** who process data on our behalf under contract,
  including **Supabase** (database, authentication, hosting), **Stripe**
  (payments), and **[email provider]** (transactional email). They may only use
  the data to provide services to us.
- **For legal reasons** — to comply with law, enforce our Terms, or protect the
  rights, safety, and property of users, the public, or CampHQ.
- **In a business transfer** — if CampHQ is involved in a merger, acquisition, or
  sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

We do **not** sell personal information.

## 7. Data retention and deletion

- A published camp is live for its **[60]-day window**; afterward it is archived
  (read-only) and then scheduled for deletion per our retention schedule unless
  the Organizer extends or exports it.
- Organizers may delete a camp, a participant record, or their account at any
  time; deletion removes the associated personal information from active systems,
  with residual copies purged from backups on a rolling basis.
- We retain limited billing records as required by law and tax rules.

## 8. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards — encryption in transit, hashed passwords,
access controls, and server-enforced permissions. No system is perfectly secure,
and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 9. Your choices and rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or
export personal information, or to object to certain processing.

- **Participants:** because the Organizer controls your camp information, please
  direct requests to your Organizer first; we will assist them.
- **Organizers:** you can manage your account data in-app or contact us.
- To exercise rights directly with us, email **[privacy@yourdomain]**.

**California residents** (CCPA/CPRA): we do not sell or "share" personal
information as those terms are defined; you may request access or deletion as
above. **E, EEA/UK residents:** where applicable, we process data as a processor
on the Organizer's instructions and rely on appropriate safeguards for any
transfers.
